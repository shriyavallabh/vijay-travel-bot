PLACES TO VISIT - DETAILED INFORMATION
================================================================================

JAIPUR - THE PINK CITY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
History: Founded in 1727 by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II
UNESCO Status: World Heritage City (2019)
Famous For: Pink-washed buildings, forts, palaces
Local Cuisine: Dal Baati Churma, Ghewar, Pyaaz Kachori
Best Souvenirs: Blue pottery, block prints, gemstones
Local Transport: Auto-rickshaws (negotiate fare)

PUSHKAR - THE SACRED TOWN
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
History: One of the oldest cities in India
Religious Significance: Only Brahma temple in world
Famous For: Pushkar Lake, Camel Fair (November)
Local Cuisine: Vegetarian only (holy city), Malpua
Best Souvenirs: Silver jewelry, leather goods
Note: Alcohol and non-veg food banned

JODHPUR - THE BLUE CITY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
History: Founded in 1459 by Rao Jodha
Famous For: Blue-painted Brahmin houses, Mehrangarh Fort
Local Cuisine: Mirchi Bada, Makhaniya Lassi, Daal Baati
Best Souvenirs: Mojari shoes, spices, antiques
Local Transport: Auto and cycle rickshaws

UDAIPUR - THE CITY OF LAKES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
History: Founded in 1559 by Maharana Udai Singh II
Famous For: Lakes, palaces, romantic ambiance
Local Cuisine: Daal Baati, Gatte ki Sabzi
Best Souvenirs: Miniature paintings, silver jewelry
James Bond Connection: Octopussy filmed at Lake Palace

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS
================================================================================

Q: What is the best time to visit Rajasthan?
A: October to March offers pleasant weather. Avoid April-June (extreme heat).

Q: Is this tour suitable for elderly travelers?
A: Yes, with some modifications. Fort visits involve walking/climbing.

Q: Can we modify the itinerary?
A: Yes, customization available with 48 hours notice.

Q: What about vegetarian food options?
A: Rajasthan has excellent vegetarian cuisine. All hotels offer veg options.

Q: Is photography allowed at monuments?
A: Yes, but no tripods. Some museums charge camera fees.

Q: How much should we budget for meals and shopping?
A: Approx Rs 3000-5000 per day for meals (lunch), shopping varies.

Q: Are the hotels centrally located?
A: Yes, all hotels are in city centers near major attractions.

Q: What if we need medical assistance?
A: All cities have good hospitals. Travel insurance recommended.
